Title: Phase-change Materials in MEMS Sensors: Expanding Functionalities

Thesis Statement: Through a strategic integration of phase-change materials (PCMs) in piezoresistive Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems (MEMS) sensors, this research aims to enhance sensor performance and multifunctionality, leveraging the unique properties of PCMs to address pressing challenges in various fields, including healthcare, aerospace, and structural monitoring.

Abstract:
In an era marked by technological advancements, the strategic integration of phase-change materials (PCMs) into Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems (MEMS) sensors represents a promising avenue for enhancing sensor performance and multifunctionality. Against this backdrop, this research explores the feasibility and potential applications of PCM-enhanced MEMS sensors, aiming to address pressing challenges in diverse fields such as healthcare, aerospace, and structural monitoring. The methodology involved theoretical frameworks and experimental methodologies for characterizing the behavior of PCM-based MEMS sensors. Findings revealed the significant potential of PCM integration in enhancing sensor sensitivity, response time, and energy efficiency. The research underscores the importance of understanding solid-to-solid phase transitions and their implications for sensor design and fabrication. The implications of this study include the development of advanced MEMS sensor platforms with improved performance and functionality, enabling innovations in areas such as remote health monitoring, environmental sensing, and aerospace technology. In conclusion, this research contributes to the advancement of sensor technology, paving the way for transformative applications in various industries.
